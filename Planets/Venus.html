Overview

Venus is often called Earth's "evil twin". While they are nearly identicalin size and structure, Venus is a world of extreme contradictions - a beautiful, brilliant point of light in our sky that hides a crushing, volcanic hellscape beneath its cloud.

  • A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus
  • When it rains on Venus, it rains Acid
  • Its rhe hottest planet in the Solar System

Feautures

The Atmosphere: A Runaway Greenhouse

Venus is the ulimate cautionary tale of climate change. Its atmosphere is 96% carbon dioxide, which acts like a heavy thermal blanket, trapping solar heat.

  • Sulfuric Acid Rain: The thick, yellowish clouds are not water vapor; they are composed of sulfaric acid. While it "rains" in the upper atmosphere, the heat is so intense that the droplets evoporate long before they hit the ground.
  • Super-Rotstion: While the planet itself spins slower than a person can walk, its upper winds whip around the planet at 360km/h, circiling the entire globe in just 4 Earth days.

    • The Surface: A Volcanic Desert

    Because the clouds are so thick, we can only see the surface using radar. What we've found is a geologically "young" surface (300 - 500 Million Years Old) dominated by:

  • Volcanoes: Venus has more volcanoes than any other planet in our solar system - over 1 600 major ones. Scientists are still debating if some are currently are currently erupting.
  • Tesserae: These are highly deformed, platue-like terrains that may be the oldest surfaces on the planet, potentially holding clues to whether Venus once had oceans.
  • The Pressure: Standing on the surface would feel like being crushed by a weight equivalent to a small car pressing down on earth every sqaure inch of your body.

    The Longest Day

    On Venus, a "day" is actually longer than a "year ".
    The rotation: It takes Venus about 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis.
    The Orbit: It only takes about 225 earth days to orbit the Sun.
    The Result: If you lived there, you could technically celebrateyour birthday more often than you'd see a single sunrise

    The Backward Spinner

    Venus is one of only 2 planets (The other planet is Uranus) that rotates clockwise, or "retrograde".
    If you could see through the thick clouds, the Sun would rise in the westand set in the East.
    Scientists believe a massive collision with an astriod billions of years ago might have knocked the planet completely upside down or reversed its spin.

  • Surface Temperature - 464°C (Hottest planet in the solar system)
  • Atmosphere Pressure - 92 bars (Equivalent to 1 km underwater on Earth)
  • Day Length - 243 Earth Days (Longer than its year)
  • Year Length - 225 Earth Days
  • Rotation - Retrograde (The Sun dises in the west)
  • Moons - None

    • It "Snows" Metal

    While Earth has water snow, Venus has a much more "heavy metal" version
    In the higher elevations and mountain peaks, radar reflections show a shiny, metalic coating.
    Scientists believe this is caused by vaporized lead sulfide and bismuth sulfide that frost onto the mountain tops, creating a metalic "snow".

    Constant Lightning

    Venus is a very noisy planet. Even though there is no water, the atmosphere is filled with constant electrical discharges.
    The lightning on Venus doesn't come from water clouds; its likely generated witin the sulfuric acid clouds.
    This creates a perpetual, eerie glow within the thick clouds layers.

    The "Windy" planet

    While the planet itself rotates very slowly, its atmosphere is in a state of super-rotation
    Winds in the upper atmosphere reach speeds of 360km/h.
    These winds circle the entire planet in just 4 days, dragging the clouds along at breakneck speeds while the rocky surface below barely moves.

    Exploration

    After decades of focusing on Mars, the late 2020s and early 2030s are being hailed as the "Decade of Venus". Several major missions are currently in development to solve tha mystery of how Vwnus turned into a "hellscape"

  • 1. DAVINCI+ (NASA): An atmosphereprobe that will plunge through the clouds to measure chemical composition and take high-resolution photos of the surface during its descent.
  • 2. VERITAS (NASA): An orbiter designed to map the entire planet's topgraphy in 3D to look for active plate tectonics and volcanism.
  • 3. EnVision (ESA): A European mission (with NASA contradictions) set to study the planet from its inner core to its upper atmosphere.
