Overview

Mercury is the smallest planet in out solar system and the closet to the Sun. Despite its proximity to our star, it is not the hottest planet, but its certainly one of the most extreme.

  • It is the 2nd densest planet, afer Earth.
  • Despite being this close to the sun, it has ice water at the poles.
  • Mercury is actaully getting smaller.

Feautures

    Orbital Dynamics and Rotation

    Mercury has a unique relastionship wit the Sun. It orbits faster than oter planet, traveling at roughly 47km/s

  • Year Length: A single orbit takes only 88 earth days
  • Day Length: Mercury rotates very slowly on its axis. One solar day (noon to noon) takes about 176 Earth Days
  • Spin-Orbit Resonance: For every two orbits around the Sun, Mercurycompletes exactly three rotations on its axis.

    • Physical Characteristics

    Mercury is a terrestrial planet with a rocky body much like Earth, but it lacks a substansial atmosphere.

  • Size: It is nly slightly larger than Earths Moon.
  • Surface: The landscape is heavily craterd and ancient, resembling the lunar surface. Notable featers include the Caloris Basin (a massive impact crater, and "lobate scraps" (cliffs) that suggest the planet shrank as its core cooled.
  • Composition: Mercury is exceptaionally dense. It has a massive metallic core - mostly iron - that makes up about 85% of the palnets radius.

    • Atmosphere and Temperature

    Because Mercury is too small and hot to hold onto a significant atmosphere, it has only a thin exosphere composed of atoms blasted off the surface by solar wind.

  • Temperature Extremes: Without an atmosphere to trap heat, temperature fluctuate wildly.
    Day side: Can reach 430C
    Night side: Casn drop to -180C

Details

    2026 Mission Update: BepiColombo

    The joint European (ESA) and Japanese(JAXA) mission, BepiColobo, is currently on its final approach to the planet.

  • Final Flyby: On Jan 8 2025, the spacecraft completed its 6th and final gravity assist around Mercury.
  • Arrival Date: It is scheduled to enter orbit in Nov 2026.
  • Objectives: It consists of 2 separate orbiters that will decouple to map Mercurys magnetic field and surface composition in unprecedented detail, investigation why such small planet has a liguid core and global magnatic field.

    Orbital Dynamics & "Retrograde"

    Mercury is famous for its speed, orbiting the Sun every 88 Earth days. However, its rotation is incredibly slow, leading to some unique phenomena:

  • A long Day: One full day-night cycle on Mercury takes 176 Earth days - exactly 2 Mercury Years!
  • Mercury Retrograde (2026): From our prespective on Earth, Mercury appears to move backward in the sky 3 - 4 times a year. in 2026, these periods occur:
    Feb 26 - Mar 20
    Jun 19 - July 23
    Oct 24 - Nov 13
  • Diameter - 4 879 km (About 38% of Earth)
    Moons - None
    Atmosphere - Thin "Exosphere" (Oxygen, Sodium, Hydrogen)
    Surface Gravity - 3.7m/sˆ2 (38% of Earths gravity)
    Color - Dark Gray/Brown (coverd in dust and silicate)

    Physical Composition & Surface

    Mercury is the second densest planet after Earth, which is surpricing given its small size.

  • The Massive Core: Approximately 75% to 85% of the planet’s radius is a metallic iron core. Scientists believe a massive collision in the distant past may have stripped away much of its original rocky crust and mantle.
  • Shrinking Planet: Mercury is actually getting smaller. As its interior cools, the planet contracts, creating "lobate scarps" or giant cliffs that can be hundreds of miles long and a mile high.
  • Ice at the Equator? No, but it exists at the poles. Despite daytime temperatures of 430°C, deep craters at the poles are in permanent shadow. These "cold traps" are cold enough to hold water ice for billions of years.

Exploration

Mercury is difficult to visit because of its proximity to the Suns gravity. Only three missions have explored it:

  • 1. Mariner 10 (1974-1975): Provided the first close-up images.
  • 2. MESSENGER (2004-2015): Mapped the entire surface and discovered the high iron content.
  • 3. BepiColombo (Launced 2018): A joint mission between ESA and JAXA currently en route to enter orbit in late 2025.
