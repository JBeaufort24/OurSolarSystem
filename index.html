Overview

Our solar system is a vast, gravitationally bound neighborhood in the Milky Way galaxy. Its about 4.6 billion years old, formed from the gravitational collapse of a giant intersteller molecular cloud

A the heart of it all is the Sun, a yellow dwarf star that contains roughtly 99.8% of the systems total mass. Its gravity is the "glue" that keeps everything - from gaint planets to the tiny debris - in orbit

  • The Sun contains 99.86% of the total mass of the entire solar system
  • A day on Venus is longer than its year
  • Jupiter is the Solar Systems vacuum cleaner
